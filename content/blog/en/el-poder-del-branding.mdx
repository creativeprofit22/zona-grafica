## A logo is not your brand

After more than 30 years designing visual identities from San Miguel de Allende, there's something we've learned: a logo is just the tip of the iceberg. Your brand is everything people feel when they interact with your business — from the color of your walls to the tone of your latest email.

At Zona Gráfica, we've seen it firsthand with every project. When we developed the identity for GIFF over more than two decades, we weren't just making "a logo for a film festival." We were building a visual language that evolved year after year, keeping its essence while reinventing itself with every edition.

## The three pillars we stand by

**Strategy first.** Before we open Illustrator, we need to understand who you are. What's your story? What sets you apart in your market? In San Miguel, where creative businesses abound, finding your authentic voice is the difference between being remembered and being just another name.

**A system, not a standalone piece.** A visual identity isn't a logo and a business card. It's a complete system: typography, color palette, photography style, tone of voice, applications. Everything works together. When we designed the brand for Cardo Café, every element — from the menu to the venue's signage — told the same story.

**Consistency over time.** The strongest brands don't change every six months. They evolve with intention. Our 22-year relationship with GIFF is proof that consistency builds real recognition.

## Context matters

Designing branding in San Miguel de Allende is different from doing it in Mexico City or New York. Here, the cultural context is incredibly rich: colonial facades, earthy tones, artisanal craftsmanship, the blend of tradition and contemporary life. The best local brands don't ignore that context — they embrace it.

When we work with tourism businesses, restaurants, or galleries, we look for the point where the identity of the business and the identity of the city meet naturally. It's not about slapping a colonial arch on everything. It's about capturing the essence of the place with sophistication.

## Your brand is an investment

Many see branding as an expense. Those who see it as an investment are the ones who end up with businesses people remember and recommend. A well-built identity works for you every day: in every Instagram post, every menu you hand out, every Google search where you show up.
